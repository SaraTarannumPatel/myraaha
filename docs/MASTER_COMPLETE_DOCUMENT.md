## Executive Summary: The Naming Decision

This document presents the complete vision for MyRaaha's career exploration module through two naming lenses: **CareerMap** and **CareerScape**. Both names capture the "Google Maps for careers" metaphor, each with distinct strengths:

### **CareerMap**
Simple, precise, self-explaining, and permanently anchored to the metaphor. When you open Google Maps, you don't need it explained. You just know what it does. CareerMap should feel exactly the same way — every 10th-standard student in Nagpur and every burnt-out software engineer in Bengaluru should open it and immediately understand: *this is the map of my career life.*

### **CareerScape** 🗺️
Works because "Scape" = Landscape View — like looking at a vast terrain from above (Google Maps satellite view). Immediately visual, suggests an immersive, explorable space (like cityscape, landscape, seascape). Self-explanatory: Career + Scape = "the entire career landscape laid out before you." Supports rich sub-features like PathView, Elevation Mode, Territory Markers (like Street View, Terrain, Pins). Modern, sticky, tech-forward, memorable, brandable.

Both names work. The choice depends on whether you prioritize **immediate clarity** (CareerMap) or **evocative imagery** (CareerScape). The features, functionality, and vision remain identical.

## Part 2: The 5 User Types — Who Enters the Map

Your documents describe 5 distinct user types. Every journey through the career exploration module is personalized from the first second based on who you are. Think of these as 5 different people opening Google Maps in 5 different countries — the map is the same, but where they are and where they want to go is completely different.

**User Type 1: The School Student (Class 10-12)**
- Standing at the first major fork in the road
- Needs to choose: Science, Commerce, Arts, or direct career entry
- Questions: "What even exists as careers?" "Which stream leads where?" "What are exam gates?"
- Map shows: All 17 sectors, entry points from each stream, exam gates marked clearly, time horizons visible

**User Type 2: The College Student**
- Already on a path but can still pivot
- Questions: "Is this the right path?" "What jobs actually exist in my field?" "Should I prepare for exams or campus placements?"
- Map shows: Roles accessible from their degree, adjacent pivots, companies hiring their profile, exam gates still available

**User Type 3: The Early Professional (0-3 years experience)**
- In first job, evaluating if it's right
- Questions: "Should I switch?" "What's the next level?" "How do I transition domains?"
- Map shows: Career progression ladders, lateral moves, upskilling gaps, cross-sector bridges via Career Clusters

**User Type 4: The Mid-Career Switcher (3+ years experience)**
- Wants change but worried about starting over
- Questions: "Can I switch without going back to zero?" "What transfers?" "How long will it take?"
- Map shows: Transfer skills analysis, fastest pivot paths, Career Clusters showing 78% skill overlap between similar roles across sectors

**User Type 5: The Aspiring Entrepreneur**
- Parallel to career paths, wants to build something
- Questions: "What should I build?" "Do I have the skills?" "What's the path from idea to launch?"
- Map shows: Startup Sparks module, ideation to scaling journey, KSAO match to founder requirements, venture paths

## Part 7: The Complete Written Vision (Plain Language)

### **The Core Metaphor**

Imagine you're standing in a new city at night. You have a destination — a restaurant, a friend's house, a hospital — but no map, no signs, no phone signal. You could walk in random directions for hours. You could ask strangers who give you conflicting advice. Or you could wander home, convinced you'll find your way tomorrow. 

This is the experience of choosing a career in India today. You are dropped into a city of 17,285 different destinations and told: pick one. Figure out how to get there yourself.

**[CareerMap/CareerScape] is the map that should have always existed.**

### **The Name**

The name — **CareerMap** or **CareerScape** — earns its place because it does not promise to guide you. It maps. It shows you the entire terrain — every sector, every role, every path between them. You get to choose where you want to go. The map just makes sure you know what you're choosing and how to get there.

**CareerMap** is simple, precise, self-explaining. When you open Google Maps, you don't need it explained. CareerMap should feel exactly the same way.

**CareerScape** evokes landscape, territory, exploration. It suggests an immersive space you can wander through, like a cityscape or seascape. It's modern, sticky, brandable.

Both work. The vision is identical.

### **When You Enter**

When you enter the module, the first thing it does is ask you who you are — not your name or your rank or your college. It asks about the 229 dimensions of what makes you, you. What energies you find fulfilling. How you think. What kind of work feels meaningful. Whether you prefer solitude or collaboration. This is the **Curiosity Compass** — a 45-minute psychometric assessment that generates your unique career GPS signal. Your 229-dimensional KSAO vector. Your starting coordinates on the map.

### **The Map Opens**

17,285 career roles, across 17 sectors of human work. Healthcare. Technology. Law. Government. Sports. Media. Agriculture. Finance. Real Estate. Education. Energy. Manufacturing. Transport. Hospitality. Retail. NGO. Telecom. 

Each sector is a district on the map. Each career role is a building in that district. The roles that match your KSAO profile glow. The ones that are far from your current position appear dimmer. You can zoom in, zoom out, search by name, search by domain. Every pin you click opens a building — and inside every building is a complete world.

### **RoleView / The Role Deep Dive**

Our version of Google's Street View — is what you see when you click any career. Not a job description. Not a Wikipedia summary. A living, 18-dimensional intelligence profile. The algorithm has mapped 400+ data points for every career. 

You learn:
- What this person does on a Tuesday
- What they earn at different stages in different cities
- Who hires them — and which companies are growing, which are shrinking
- How AI is affecting this career specifically
- What the 6-year career ladder looks like
- What examination gates stand between you and this role
- Which colleges in India can best prepare you
- How this role compares to three similar alternatives
- And — the feature that no other platform has ever built — **your personal match score**: "You are 88% matched to this role. Here is exactly what you're strong in. Here are the 3 gaps you need to close."

You can watch a 360° video of someone doing this job. You can take a virtual tour of their workspace. You can try solving a real problem from their daily work. You can hear from real professionals: "Here's what they don't tell you about being a Management Consultant."

### **PathFinder / The Road Planner**

Our version of route navigation — takes your destination and draws the entire journey. 

If you're in Class 10 and you want to be an IAS Officer, the system shows you: 
- You need to choose Science or Arts (it recommends based on your KSAO)
- You need to graduate from any accredited college
- You need to pass the UPSC Civil Services Examination — the algorithm marks this as a **mandatory Gate**, a red traffic light that cannot be bypassed, and it flags this for you in Class 10, not after your graduation, when it's too late
- It shows you the three-stage UPSC process
- It tells you the approximate time required
- It tells you which cities have the best UPSC coaching ecosystems
- And it tells you: you have 8 years from today to this destination. Here is what you do each year.

You get **three route options** for every destination:
1. **Fastest Path** — shortest timeline, higher cost, intense difficulty
2. **Safest Path** — longer timeline, lower risk, proven track record
3. **No-Cost Path** — self-taught emphasis, maximum time investment, free resources

Each route shows:
- Timeline (months or years)
- Cost estimate (₹)
- Difficulty rating (⭐)
- Step-by-step milestones
- Live market updates (hiring spikes reduce ETA, scholarships reduce cost)

### **The Exam Gate System**

One of the module's most important features, and one that no other platform has ever built seriously. 

India's career architecture has something that most of the Western world does not — **mandatory, non-negotiable examination gates** that stand between a person and their career. 

NEET for doctors. JEE for engineers. CLAT for lawyers. CA Foundation, Intermediate, and Final for chartered accountants. UPSC for civil servants. GATE for PSU engineers. NDA for Army officers. IBPS for bank officers. 

**35+ gates, all mapped, all labeled as mandatory waypoints.**

The system shows these gates **before you commit to a path**. It warns you in advance. It builds preparation into your road plan. It treats them exactly as Google Maps treats a toll booth — you will encounter this. Here is when. Here is how much it will cost you. Here is how to prepare.

When you clear a gate, you earn a **Gate Crasher badge**. The system celebrates. It suggests a mentor who cleared the same gate. It opens the next stage of your journey.

### **The Career Knowledge Graph**

The actual map rendering — is a live, interactive D3.js visualization where **every career is a node and every relationship between careers is a drawn edge**. 

Adjacent roles are connected. Career progressions are drawn as upward paths. Cross-sector switches are drawn as bridges. │  │    - User can interact before full load                    │         │

│  │ 5. WebGL Rendering (for 10K+ nodes)                        │         │

## The Competitive Moat

**Moat 1: Data Depth**
400+ fields per role is not something a competitor can replicate in 6 months or 12 months. This is years of research, interviews, partnerships, and algorithm development. 17,285 roles is not "we scraped LinkedIn." This is structured, verified, maintained data.

**Moat 2: The Algorithm**
229-dimensional KSAO matching is not keyword search. It is psychometric science. It is machine learning on personality-to-career fit. It is years of research translated into code. Career Clusters identification — finding 78% skill overlap between careers across sectors — is novel research. This has not been mapped before. Exam Gate integration into pathfinding is India-specific intelligence that foreign competitors cannot replicate.

**Moat 3: The Network Effect**
Reality Checks get better as more professionals contribute videos. Mentor matching improves as the mentor pool grows. Live Hiring Pulse gets more accurate as more users provide signal. Community-generated collections compound over time. The product gets better with every user. This is a network effect.

**Moat 4: The Experience**
D3.js Career Knowledge Graph is not a feature you add in a sprint. It is months of visualization engineering. 360° RoleView videos require production partnerships and infrastructure. Gamification system with XP, badges, levels, Career Passport — this is game design expertise applied to career navigation. Competitors can copy the idea. They cannot easily copy the execution.

**Moat 5: The Integration**
The value is not in any single module. It is in how 7 modules integrate seamlessly: SelfGraph → Curiosity Compass → Career Map → PathFinder → SkillStacker → Living Resume → Mentor Matching → Startup Sparks. Breaking one link breaks the magic. Competitors would need to build the entire ecosystem to compete.
